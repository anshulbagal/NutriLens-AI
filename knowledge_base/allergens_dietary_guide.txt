FOOD ALLERGENS AND DIETARY RESTRICTIONS GUIDE
==============================================

THE BIG 9 MAJOR FOOD ALLERGENS (USA - FASTER Act 2023)
1. Milk (Dairy)
2. Eggs
3. Fish
4. Shellfish (Crustacean)
5. Tree Nuts
6. Peanuts
7. Wheat
8. Soybeans
9. Sesame (Added 2023)

THE BIG 14 ALLERGENS (European Union)
1. Celery
2. Cereals containing gluten (wheat, rye, barley, oats, spelt, kamut)
3. Crustaceans
4. Eggs
5. Fish
6. Lupin
7. Milk
8. Molluscs
9. Mustard
10. Peanuts
11. Sesame seeds
12. Soybeans
13. Sulphur dioxide and sulphites (>10ppm)
14. Tree Nuts

---
DETAILED ALLERGEN PROFILES
---

MILK / DAIRY ALLERGY
Hidden names to watch for:
- Butter, butterfat, buttermilk, butter oil
- Casein, caseinate, rennet casein
- Cheese (all types), cream, cream cheese, curds
- Ghee, half-and-half, lactalbumin, lactoferrin
- Lactoglobulin, lactose, lactulose
- Milk (all forms: condensed, derivative, dry, evaporated, skim, solids, whole)
- Nougat, pudding, recaldent, sour cream, whey (all forms)
- Yogurt
- May contain: caramel, chocolate, deli meats (some), luncheon meat, margarine, non-dairy products (paradoxically)

LACTOSE INTOLERANCE vs DAIRY ALLERGY:
- Lactose intolerance: inability to digest lactose (milk sugar); GI symptoms only
- Dairy allergy: immune reaction to milk proteins (casein or whey); can be life-threatening
- People with lactose intolerance can often tolerate: hard cheese, butter, yogurt (low lactose)
- People with dairy allergy must avoid ALL dairy including trace amounts

EGG ALLERGY
Hidden names:
- Albumin, egg (dried, powdered, solids, white, yolk), eggnog
- Globulin, livetin, lysozyme, mayonnaise, meringue, ovalbumin
- Ovomucin, ovomucoid, ovotransferrin, ovovitellin, silici albuminate
- Surimi (sometimes), Simplesse (fat replacer)
- May contain: some pastas (egg pasta), processed meats, marshmallows, marzipan

PEANUT ALLERGY
- One of the most common and severe food allergies
- Can cause anaphylaxis; potentially life-threatening
- Hidden names: Beer nuts, boiled peanuts, cold pressed peanut oil, earth nuts
- Ground nuts, mandelonas, mixed nuts, monkey nuts, Nu-Nuts
- Peanut butter, peanut flour, peanut protein hydrolysate
- May contain: African, Asian, Mexican cuisines (common use), cereals, candy, egg rolls, mole sauce
- Cross-reactive: NOT related to tree nuts biologically, but often processed in same facilities

TREE NUT ALLERGY
Types: Almonds, Brazil nuts, Cashews, Chestnuts, Filberts/Hazelnuts, Hickory nuts, Macadamia nuts, Pecans, Pine nuts (Pinon), Pistachios, Shea nuts, Walnuts
Hidden names:
- Almond paste, artificial nuts, beechnut, butternut, chinquapin
- Gianduja (chocolate-nut mixture), ginkgo nut, litchi/lychee nut
- Marzipan/almond paste, nangai nut, natural nut extract, nut meal
- Nut meat, nut pieces, pesto (often pine nuts), praline
- Coconut: FDA classifies as tree nut; many tree nut-allergic tolerate coconut

WHEAT ALLERGY vs CELIAC DISEASE vs NON-CELIAC GLUTEN SENSITIVITY:
- Wheat allergy: IgE-mediated immune response to wheat proteins; can be anaphylactic
- Celiac disease: autoimmune disorder triggered by gluten; damages small intestine villi
- Non-celiac gluten sensitivity (NCGS): symptoms without celiac autoimmunity or wheat allergy

WHEAT / GLUTEN ALLERGY
Gluten-containing grains: Wheat, Barley, Rye, Triticale, Spelt, Kamut, Durum, Einkorn, Emmer, Farro
Hidden names for wheat:
- Bread crumbs, bulgur, cereal extract, dinkel, durum, einkorn
- Emmer, farina, faro, flour (all-purpose, enriched, graham, high gluten, high protein)
- Kamut, malt, matzoh/matzah, semolina, spelt, triticale, wheat (all forms)
- Wheat bran, wheat germ, wheat gluten, wheat starch, whole wheat flour
Hidden gluten sources:
- Soy sauce, most soy sauces (use tamari or coconut aminos instead)
- Beer and ales (use gluten-free beer)
- Salad dressings, sauces, gravies (as thickener)
- Seasonings and spice mixes
- Medications and supplements (as binder)
- Oats: naturally gluten-free but often cross-contaminated; celiac patients need certified gluten-free oats

SOY ALLERGY
Hidden names:
- Edamame, miso, natto, shoyu, soy (milk, sauce, flour, nuts), tempeh, tofu
- Textured soy protein (TSP), textured vegetable protein (TVP)
- Soya, soybean (curd, granules), tamari (check label)
Note: Highly refined soy oil and soy lecithin may not trigger reactions in many soy-allergic individuals

FISH ALLERGY
Types: Anchovy, Bass, Catfish, Cod, Flounder, Grouper, Haddock, Hake, Halibut, Herring, Mahi-mahi, Perch, Pollock, Salmon, Scrod, Shark, Sole, Swordfish, Tilapia, Trout, Tuna
Hidden sources:
- Caesar salad dressing (anchovies), Worcestershire sauce, imitation fish or shellfish
- Asian sauces (fish sauce, oyster sauce)
- Barbecue sauce, bouillabaisse
- Miso soup (sometimes), gelatin (sometimes fish-derived)
Note: Fish and shellfish are separate allergens — can be allergic to one and not the other

SHELLFISH ALLERGY
Two types:
1. Crustaceans: Crab, Crawfish/Crayfish, Lobster, Prawns, Shrimp
2. Mollusks: Abalone, Clams, Cockles, Mussels, Octopus, Oysters, Scallops, Snails, Squid
Hidden sources:
- Bouillabaisse, cuttlefish ink, glucosamine (from shellfish shells)
- Seafood flavoring, surimi (imitation crabmeat), tom yum paste

SESAME ALLERGY (USA - new 2023)
Hidden names:
- Benne, benne seed, benniseed, gingelly oil, gingelly, gomasio (sesame salt)
- Halvah, sesame flour, sesame oil, sesame paste, sesame salt, sesame seed
- Sesame seed oil, sim sim, tahini, til, tilseed
Found in: Middle Eastern, Asian, and African foods; hummus, tahini, baked goods, snack foods, dressings

CROSS-REACTIVITY AND CROSS-CONTAMINATION
---

CROSS-REACTIVITY (Allergic to similar proteins across foods):
- Latex-fruit syndrome: latex allergy → cross-react with banana, avocado, kiwi, chestnut
- Pollen-food allergy syndrome: birch pollen → apple, pear, cherry, peach, plum, almond, carrot, celery, potato
- Shellfish → dust mites (similar tropomyosin protein)
- Peach allergy → other stone fruits (apricot, cherry, plum, nectarine)

CROSS-CONTAMINATION on labels:
- "May contain [allergen]": manufactured on equipment that also processes allergen
- "Made in a facility that also processes [allergen]": shared building
- "Made on shared equipment with [allergen]": same production line
- These are voluntary advisory labels — mandatory allergen declarations are separate

DIETARY RESTRICTIONS REFERENCE
---

VEGAN HIDDEN ANIMAL INGREDIENTS:
- Carmine (E120) - red dye from cochineal insects
- Gelatin - from animal bones/skin; found in marshmallows, gummy candy, Jello, some yogurts
- Whey - milk protein; found in many protein bars, crackers
- Casein - milk protein; found in some "non-dairy" creamers
- Lactose - milk sugar; found in some medications, breads
- Honey - from bees
- Lanolin (Vitamin D3) - from sheep wool; many Vitamin D supplements
- L-cysteine - from duck/chicken feathers; used in bread dough
- Rennet - from animal stomachs; used in cheese
- Shellac (E904) - from lac bugs; used as candy coating
- Isinglass - from fish bladder; used to clarify beer and wine
- Beeswax (E901) - used in candy coating and wax

HALAL RESTRICTIONS:
- Prohibited (Haram): Pork and pork derivatives (gelatin, lard, rennet), alcohol, blood products, carnivorous animals, animals not slaughtered per halal method
- Questionable: Gelatin (must be halal-certified), vanilla extract (contains alcohol), enzymes (source matters), emulsifiers from animal fat

KOSHER RESTRICTIONS:
- Prohibited: Pork, shellfish, mixing meat and dairy
- Slaughter: Must be performed by a trained shochet
- Pareve: Contains neither meat nor dairy (fish, eggs, plants)
- Kosher certification symbols: OU, OK, Star-K, Kof-K

FODMAP (for IBS):
High FODMAP foods to limit:
- Oligosaccharides: Wheat, rye, onions, garlic, legumes
- Disaccharides: Lactose (milk, yogurt, soft cheese)
- Monosaccharides: Excess fructose (apples, honey, high-fructose corn syrup)
- Polyols: Sorbitol, mannitol, xylitol, maltitol, stone fruits, cauliflower, mushrooms

READING LABELS FOR ALLERGENS
---

US FDA REQUIREMENTS:
- Top 9 allergens must be declared in plain language
- Can appear in ingredient list (e.g., "flour (wheat)") or in a separate "Contains" statement
- "Contains: Milk, Eggs, Wheat" appears after ingredients list

EU REQUIREMENTS:
- Top 14 allergens must be emphasized in ingredient list (bold, italic, or underlined)
- Must be declared even in compound ingredients

INDIA FSSAI REQUIREMENTS:
- Must declare: peanuts, tree nuts, milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, soy, wheat/gluten, sulfites (>10ppm)
- Must appear in bold in ingredients list

TIPS FOR ALLERGEN AVOIDANCE:
1. Always read the full ingredient list, not just the "Contains" box
2. Check for advisory statements ("May contain")
3. Be aware of hidden names for allergens
4. Contact manufacturer if uncertain
5. When eating out: inform staff of allergies, ask about cross-contamination
6. Carry epinephrine auto-injector if prescribed for anaphylaxis risk
7. Re-read labels every time — manufacturers change formulations
