FOOD ADDITIVES AND E-NUMBERS REFERENCE GUIDE
=============================================

WHAT ARE FOOD ADDITIVES?
Food additives are substances added to food during processing to preserve flavor,
enhance taste, improve appearance, extend shelf life, or improve food safety.
They are regulated by FDA (USA), EFSA (Europe), and FSSAI (India).

E-NUMBERS are codes assigned by the European Union to approved food additives.
An E-number guarantees the additive has been assessed and approved for use in the EU.

---
COLORS (E100–E199)
---

E100 Curcumin (Turmeric) - Natural yellow color from turmeric root. Antioxidant properties. Safe.
E101 Riboflavin (Vitamin B2) - Natural yellow/orange color. Also a nutrient. Safe.
E102 Tartrazine - Synthetic yellow dye. May cause hyperactivity in children sensitive to it. Banned in some countries.
E104 Quinoline Yellow - Synthetic yellow. Restricted in USA, Japan, Australia.
E110 Sunset Yellow FCF - Synthetic orange-yellow. May cause hyperactivity. Requires warning label in EU.
E120 Cochineal / Carmine - Red color derived from insects. Halal/vegan concern. Can cause allergic reactions.
E122 Carmoisine - Synthetic red. Requires warning in EU. Banned in USA, Japan.
E123 Amaranth - Synthetic dark red. Banned in USA since 1976.
E124 Ponceau 4R - Synthetic red. Banned in USA. Requires EU warning.
E127 Erythrosine - Synthetic cherry-red. FDA-approved. Contains iodine.
E129 Allura Red AC (Red 40) - Synthetic red. Most widely used red dye in USA. Hyperactivity concerns in children.
E131 Patent Blue V - Synthetic blue. Banned in USA, Australia. Rarely used.
E132 Indigo Carmine (Blue 2) - Synthetic blue. FDA-approved. Generally safe.
E133 Brilliant Blue FCF (Blue 1) - Synthetic blue. FDA-approved.
E140 Chlorophylls - Natural green color from plants. Safe.
E150a Caramel Color (plain) - Heated sugar. Used in cola, beer, soy sauce. Safe.
E150d Caramel Color (sulfite-ammonia) - Contains 4-methylimidazole (4-MEI), a potential carcinogen. Common in cola drinks.
E160a Beta-carotene - Natural orange, precursor to Vitamin A. Safe and beneficial.
E160b Annatto - Natural yellow-orange from seeds. Can cause allergic reactions.
E171 Titanium Dioxide - White color. European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) concluded it's no longer safe (2021). Banned in EU food.
E172 Iron Oxides and Hydroxides - Natural mineral colors (yellow, red, black). Safe.

PRESERVATIVES (E200–E299)
---

E200 Sorbic Acid - Prevents mold in cheese, baked goods, beverages. Generally safe.
E202 Potassium Sorbate - Salt of sorbic acid. Widely used. Can cause mild skin/eye irritation at high doses.
E210 Benzoic Acid - Prevents yeast and bacteria growth. Occurs naturally in cranberries.
E211 Sodium Benzoate - Combines with Vitamin C to form benzene (a carcinogen). Controversial.
E220 Sulfur Dioxide - Preservative in wine, dried fruits. Causes reactions in asthmatics (avoid if asthmatic).
E221-E228 Sulfites - Same concern as SO2; must be declared on labels if >10ppm.
E249 Potassium Nitrite - Cured meats. Forms carcinogenic nitrosamines at high temperatures.
E250 Sodium Nitrite - Bacon, ham, hot dogs, deli meats. Carcinogenic concern; necessary to prevent botulism.
E251 Sodium Nitrate - Similar to sodium nitrite. Longer-acting preservative in cured meats.
E260 Acetic Acid (Vinegar) - Safe natural preservative.
E270 Lactic Acid - Safe. Natural fermentation product.
E280 Propionic Acid - Prevents mold in bread. Generally safe.
E300 Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C) - Antioxidant preservative. Safe and nutritious.
E306-E309 Tocopherols (Vitamin E) - Natural antioxidants. Safe and beneficial.
E319 TBHQ (tert-Butylhydroquinone) - Synthetic antioxidant in fast food, chips. High doses toxic in animals; FDA limits to 0.02% of fat content.
E320 BHA (Butylated Hydroxyanisole) - Synthetic antioxidant. Possible carcinogen (California Prop 65 listed). FDA considers GRAS.
E321 BHT (Butylated Hydroxytoluene) - Synthetic antioxidant. Similar concerns to BHA. Banned in some countries.

---
ANTIOXIDANTS AND ACIDITY REGULATORS (E300–E399)
---

E330 Citric Acid - Natural, found in citrus fruit. Safe and widely used. Adds tartness.
E331 Sodium Citrates - Acidity regulator. Safe.
E332 Potassium Citrates - Safe emulsifier and acidity regulator.
E333 Calcium Citrates - Safe. Used in cheese making.
E334 Tartaric Acid - Natural in grapes. Safe.
E338 Phosphoric Acid - Found in cola drinks. Contributes to dental erosion and may affect calcium absorption.
E339 Sodium Phosphates - Emulsifier, preservative. Excessive phosphate intake linked to kidney and cardiovascular issues.

---
THICKENERS, STABILIZERS, AND EMULSIFIERS (E400–E499)
---

E401-E405 Alginates - Derived from seaweed. Safe thickeners.
E407 Carrageenan - Seaweed extract used in dairy alternatives. Some controversy over gut inflammation; generally considered safe by FDA.
E410 Locust Bean Gum - Natural from carob seeds. Safe thickener.
E412 Guar Gum - From guar beans. Safe. High doses may cause GI upset.
E415 Xanthan Gum - Fermentation product. Safe. Used in gluten-free baking.
E420 Sorbitol - Sugar alcohol sweetener. Can cause bloating at high doses.
E421 Mannitol - Sugar alcohol. Laxative effect at doses >20g.
E422 Glycerol (Glycerine) - Safe humectant and solvent.
E440 Pectin - Natural from fruit peels. Safe. Also a fiber source.
E450 Diphosphates - Emulsifiers in processed cheese, bakery. High intake raises phosphate concerns.
E451 Triphosphates - Same concerns as E450.
E471 Mono and Diglycerides of Fatty Acids - Emulsifiers in bread, margarine. Safe; can contain trans fats.
E472e DATEM - Diacetyl tartaric acid ester of mono/diglycerides. Emulsifier in bread. Safe.
E481 Sodium Stearoyl Lactylate - Emulsifier in baked goods. Safe.
E491 Sorbitan Monostearate - Emulsifier. Safe.

---
FLAVOR ENHANCERS (E600–E699)
---

E620 Glutamic Acid - Naturally found in tomatoes, parmesan, mushrooms. Safe.
E621 Monosodium Glutamate (MSG) - Umami flavor enhancer. FDA considers GRAS. "MSG symptom complex" not confirmed in double-blind studies. High sodium content is a concern.
E627 Disodium Guanylate - Synergistic with MSG. Avoid if gout-prone (purines). Contains no MSG itself.
E631 Disodium Inosinate - Synergistic with MSG. Same purine concerns.
E635 Disodium 5'-ribonucleotides - Combination of E627+E631. Purine concern.

---
SWEETENERS (E950–E969)
---

E950 Acesulfame Potassium (Ace-K) - 200x sweeter than sugar. FDA-approved. Stable at high temps.
E951 Aspartame - 200x sweeter. Breaks down to phenylalanine, aspartic acid, methanol. Dangerous for PKU patients. FDA-approved. 40mg/kg ADI.
E952 Cyclamate - 30-50x sweeter. Banned in USA since 1969 (cancer concerns). Allowed in EU.
E954 Saccharin - 300x sweeter. Once suspected carcinogen (removed from US list in 2000). FDA-approved.
E955 Sucralose (Splenda) - 600x sweeter. Chlorinated sugar derivative. FDA-approved. Heat stable.
E960 Steviol Glycosides (Stevia) - Plant-derived. Generally recognized as safe (GRAS). 250-300x sweeter.
E961 Neotame - 7000-13000x sweeter. FDA-approved. Like aspartame but safe for PKU.
E965 Maltitol - Sugar alcohol. 75-90% sweetness of sugar. Significant GI effects at high doses.
E967 Xylitol - Sugar alcohol. 100% sweetness of sugar. Safe for humans; TOXIC TO DOGS. Dental health benefits.
E968 Erythritol - Sugar alcohol. 70% sweetness. Best tolerated; minimal GI effects. Nearly zero calories.

COMMON FOOD ADDITIVE CONCERNS BY HEALTH CONDITION
---

ASTHMA / SULFITE SENSITIVITY:
- Avoid: E220 (Sulfur Dioxide), E221-E228 (Sulfites), E102 (Tartrazine)
- Found in: wine, dried fruits, processed meats, some medications

ATTENTION DEFICIT / HYPERACTIVITY (Children):
- Avoid: E102 (Tartrazine), E104, E110, E122, E124, E129 (Red 40), E211 (Sodium Benzoate)
- "Southampton Six" colors require EU warning: "may have an adverse effect on activity and attention in children"

CARDIOVASCULAR DISEASE:
- Avoid: E250/E251 (nitrites), high-sodium preservatives, BHA/BHT, phosphates
- Limit: E320/E321, carrageenan

DIABETES / BLOOD SUGAR:
- Sugar alcohols (E420, E421, E965-E968) are safer than sugar
- Artificial sweeteners generally don't raise blood sugar
- Watch for hidden sugars in "sugar-free" products

KIDNEY DISEASE:
- Avoid: high phosphate additives (E450, E451, E452, E338, E339)
- Avoid: high potassium additives
- Limit sodium from all sources

PHENYLKETONURIA (PKU):
- MUST AVOID: E951 (Aspartame) — produces phenylalanine
- Products with aspartame must carry "Contains phenylalanine" warning

VEGAN / VEGETARIAN:
- Animal-derived additives: E120 (Carmine — from insects), E441 (Gelatin), E542 (bone phosphate), E631 (from meat/fish), E901 (beeswax), E904 (shellac from insects)

HALAL CONCERNS:
- E120 Carmine (from insects), E441 Gelatin (pork/non-halal slaughter), E470-E478 (fatty acid origin), E542 (bone phosphate)

HIDDEN NAMES FOR COMMON ADDITIVES
---

MSG appears as: Autolyzed yeast, hydrolyzed protein, yeast extract, soy protein extract, natural flavor (sometimes)
Trans fat appears as: Partially hydrogenated oils, hydrogenated vegetable shortening
Added sugar appears as: Sucrose, glucose, fructose, dextrose, maltose, lactose, corn syrup, high-fructose corn syrup, agave, honey, molasses, cane juice, fruit juice concentrate
Sodium appears as: Salt, sodium chloride, sodium bicarbonate, monosodium glutamate, sodium nitrate, disodium phosphate, brine
Sulfites appear as: Sulfur dioxide, sodium sulfite, sodium bisulfite, sodium metabisulfite, potassium bisulfite, potassium metabisulfite
